QUESTION
A cylindrical fuse wire of length$L$and radius$r$is connected across a fixed potential difference$V$. The time taken for the wire to melt is$t$. A second wire is made from the same material, but all its linear dimensions are multiplied by a factor$k$. The same potential difference$V$is applied across the second wire. Assuming that all electrical energy is converted into internal energy of the wire and that heat loss to the surroundings is negligible, what is the time taken for the second wire to melt?
OPTIONS
A: $t$
B: $k t$
C: $k^2 t$ âœ“ CORRECT
D: $k^3 t$
E: $k^4 t$

Correct Answer: C

SOLUTION
Reasoning:

Let the original wire have length$L$and radius$r$. The new wire has length$L' = kL$and radius$r' = kr$. First, consider the mass of the wire, which determines the energy required to melt it. The volume is$Vol = \pi r^2 L$. The new volume is$Vol' = \pi (kr)^2 (kL) = k^3 (\pi r^2 L) = k^3 Vol$. Since the material is the same, the mass$m$, and hence the energy$E$required to melt the wire, scales by$k^3$:$$E' = k^3 E$$Next, consider the electrical resistance$R = \rho \frac{L}{A} = \rho \frac{L}{\pi r^2}$. The new resistance is:$$R' = \rho \frac{kL}{\pi (kr)^2} = \rho \frac{kL}{k^2 \pi r^2} = \frac{1}{k} R$$Now consider the power dissipated. Since the potential difference$V$is fixed, we use$P = \frac{V^2}{R}$. The new power is:$$P' = \frac{V^2}{R'} = \frac{V^2}{R/k} = k P$$Finally, the time taken is$t = \frac{E}{P}$. The new time is:$$t' = \frac{E'}{P'} = \frac{k^3 E}{k P} = k^2 \left( \frac{E}{P} \right) = k^2 t$$Hence the correct answer is$k^2 t$.
Key Insight:

Resistance scales as$L^{-1}$when all dimensions scale uniformly, while mass scales as$L^3$; with fixed voltage, power scales as$L^1$.
