A particle of mass$m$moves along the$x$-axis. Its kinetic energy$E_k$varies with displacement$x$according to the equation:$$E_k = 6x^2 - 2x^3$$for the interval$0 \le x \le 2$. At which position$x$is the magnitude of the net force acting on the particle at its maximum?

Correct Answer: B

SOLUTION
Reasoning:

The Work-Energy theorem states that the net work done is the change in kinetic energy. In differential form, the net force$F$is the gradient of the kinetic energy with respect to position:$$F = \frac{dE_k}{dx}$$Differentiating the given expression for$E_k$:$$F = \frac{d}{dx}(6x^2 - 2x^3) = 12x - 6x^2$$We seek the position where the magnitude of the force is maximum. The function$F(x) = 12x - 6x^2$describes a parabola opening downwards. The maximum value occurs at the vertex. We find the stationary point of$F$by differentiating it:$$\frac{dF}{dx} = 12 - 12x$$Setting$\frac{dF}{dx} = 0$gives:$$12(1 - x) = 0 \implies x = 1$$Checking the values at the boundaries and the vertex: - At$x=0$,$F = 0$. - At$x=1$,$F = 12(1) - 6(1)^2 = 6$. - At$x=2$,$F = 12(2) - 6(4) = 0$. Thus, the maximum magnitude of the force occurs at$x = 1.0$. (Note: This position corresponds to the inflection point of the$E_k$graph, where the slope is steepest.)
