QUESTION
Three identical resistors are connected in a circuit with a DC power supply. One resistor is connected in series with a parallel combination of the other two. What fraction of the total power supplied by the source is dissipated by the parallel combination?
OPTIONS
A: $\frac{1}{9}$
B: $\frac{1}{6}$
C: $\frac{1}{4}$
D: $\frac{1}{3}$ âœ“ CORRECT
E: $\frac{1}{2}$
F: $\frac{2}{3}$

Correct Answer: D

SOLUTION
Reasoning:

Let the resistance of each resistor be$R$. The circuit consists of a single resistor ($R_1$) in series with a parallel pair ($R_2$and$R_3$). First, we find the equivalent resistance of the parallel combination ($R_p$):$$R_p = \left( \frac{1}{R} + \frac{1}{R} \right)^{-1} = \frac{R}{2}$$The total resistance of the circuit ($R_{total}$) is the sum of the series resistor and the parallel combination:$$R_{total} = R + \frac{R}{2} = \frac{3R}{2}$$Since the single resistor and the parallel combination are in series, the total source current$I$flows through both blocks. Using the power formula$P = I^2 R$, we can compare the power dissipated by the parallel combination ($P_p$) to the total power ($P_{total}$):$$P_p = I^2 R_p = I^2 \left( \frac{R}{2} \right)$$$$P_{total} = I^2 R_{total} = I^2 \left( \frac{3R}{2} \right)$$The fraction of the total power is:$$\frac{P_p}{P_{total}} = \frac{I^2 (R/2)}{I^2 (3R/2)} = \frac{1/2}{3/2} = \frac{1}{3}$$Hence the correct answer is D.
Key Insight:

For components connected in series, current is common, so power dissipated is directly proportional to resistance ($P \propto R$).
