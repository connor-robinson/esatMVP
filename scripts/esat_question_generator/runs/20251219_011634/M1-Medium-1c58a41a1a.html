Two cylinders,$P$and$Q$, have the same volume. The height of cylinder$Q$is twice the height of cylinder$P$. What is the ratio of the curved surface area of cylinder$Q$to the curved surface area of cylinder$P$?

Correct Answer: D

SOLUTION
Reasoning:

Let$r_P, h_P$and$r_Q, h_Q$be the radii and heights of the cylinders respectively. The curved surface area is given by$A = 2\pi rh$and the volume by$V = \pi r^2 h$. We are given$h_Q = 2h_P$and$V_P = V_Q$. Equating the volumes:$$\pi r_P^2 h_P = \pi r_Q^2 h_Q$$Substituting$h_Q = 2h_P$:$$\pi r_P^2 h_P = \pi r_Q^2 (2h_P)$$Dividing both sides by$\pi h_P$:$$r_P^2 = 2r_Q^2 \implies r_Q = \frac{r_P}{\sqrt{2}}$$Now consider the ratio of the curved surface areas:$$\frac{A_Q}{A_P} = \frac{2\pi r_Q h_Q}{2\pi r_P h_P}$$Substitute$r_Q = \frac{r_P}{\sqrt{2}}$and$h_Q = 2h_P$:$$\frac{A_Q}{A_P} = \frac{ \left( \frac{r_P}{\sqrt{2}} \right) (2h_P) }{ r_P h_P } = \frac{2}{\sqrt{2}} = \sqrt{2}$$Hence the correct answer is$\sqrt{2}$.
