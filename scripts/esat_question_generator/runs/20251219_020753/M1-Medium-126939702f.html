QUESTION
The cost of manufacturing a storage tank is proportional to its surface area. A spherical tank with a capacity of$V$costs$C$to manufacture. A second spherical tank has a capacity of$8V$. What is the cost to manufacture the second tank?
OPTIONS
A: $2C$
B: $2\sqrt{2}C$

D: $8C$
E: $16\sqrt{2}C$
F: $64C$

Correct Answer: C

SOLUTION
Reasoning:

Let the radius of the first tank be$r_1$and the radius of the second tank be$r_2$. Capacity is equivalent to volume. For a sphere, volume$V \propto r^3$. The volume of the second tank is$8$times that of the first, so:$$\left( \frac{r_2}{r_1} \right)^3 = 8$$Taking the cube root, we find the ratio of the radii (the linear scale factor):$$\frac{r_2}{r_1} = 2$$Cost is proportional to surface area$A$. For a sphere,$A \propto r^2$. The ratio of the costs is therefore the square of the linear scale factor:$$\frac{\text{Cost}_2}{\text{Cost}_1} = \left( \frac{r_2}{r_1} \right)^2 = 2^2 = 4$$Thus, the new cost is$4C$.
Key Insight:

For geometrically similar solids, if volume scales by a factor$k$, surface area scales by$k^{2/3}$.
