The line with equation$y = 2 - x$forms a triangle with the coordinate axes. A vertical line with equation$x = k$divides the area of this triangle into two equal parts. What is the value of$k$?

Correct Answer: C

SOLUTION
Reasoning:

The line$y = 2 - x$intersects the axes at$(0,2)$and$(2,0)$. The region bounded by the axes and the line is a right-angled triangle with base length$2$and height$2$. The vertical line$x = k$divides this triangle into two regions: a trapezium to the left (from$x=0$to$x=k$) and a smaller triangle to the right (from$x=k$to$x=2$). Consider the smaller triangle on the right. Its vertices are$(k,0)$,$(2,0)$, and$(k, 2-k)$. This smaller triangle is similar to the original large triangle. We require the area of the smaller triangle to be half the area of the large triangle. For similar shapes, the ratio of areas is the square of the ratio of corresponding lengths (the linear scale factor$r$).$$r^2 = \frac{\text{Area}_{\text{small}}}{\text{Area}_{\text{large}}} = \frac{1}{2}$$$$r = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}$$The base of the large triangle has length$2$. The base of the small triangle has length$2 - k$.$$\frac{2 - k}{2} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}$$$$2 - k = \frac{2}{\sqrt{2}} = \sqrt{2}$$$$k = 2 - \sqrt{2}$$Hence the correct answer is$2 - \sqrt{2}$.
Key Insight:

Working with the similar triangle at the "tip" is faster than integrating the trapezium area; area scales with the square of the linear dimension.
