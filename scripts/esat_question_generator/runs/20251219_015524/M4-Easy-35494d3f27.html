The line with equation$$2kx + (k-1)y = 2k^2 - 2k$$where$k > 1$is a constant, forms a triangle with the coordinate axes. The area of this triangle is$12$. What is the value of$k$?

Correct Answer: C

SOLUTION
Reasoning:

We determine the$x$- and$y$-intercepts of the line by setting$y=0$and$x=0$respectively. When$y=0$:$$2kx = 2k^2 - 2k$$$$2kx = 2k(k-1)$$Assuming$k \neq 0$(since$k>1$), we divide by$2k$:$$x = k-1$$When$x=0$:$$(k-1)y = 2k^2 - 2k$$$$(k-1)y = 2k(k-1)$$Since$k>1$,$k-1 \neq 0$, so we divide by$(k-1)$:$$y = 2k$$The area of the triangle formed with the axes is given by$\frac{1}{2} |x_{\text{int}}| |y_{\text{int}}|$. Since$k>1$, both intercepts are positive.$$\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} (k-1)(2k) = k(k-1)$$We are given that the area is$12$:$$k(k-1) = 12$$$$k^2 - k - 12 = 0$$$$(k-4)(k+3) = 0$$The solutions are$k=4$or$k=-3$. Since$k>1$, we must have$k=4$.
